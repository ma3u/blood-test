### Notes for Use:
- These values are based on the optimal target ranges for prevention and modern molecular medicine as recommended by Dr. Strunz and Dr. Orfanos-Boeckel. These targets are often above classic statistical laboratory reference ranges, as they are aimed at optimal metabolic function and prevention.
- Values may vary depending on the laboratory and individual situation. For individual interpretation, always consider personal history and clinical context.
- For hormones, consider cycle phase, age, and possible hormone therapy, especially in women.
- **Amino acids**: For optimal health, each amino acid should be in the upper half of the reference range; values are based on healthy, high-performing individuals and not on the average sick population.
- **Special vitamins**: Target values are often higher than standard reference ranges to support prevention and optimal function.
- **Inflammation markers**: Lower values are generally better; chronic low-grade inflammation is a risk factor for many diseases.
- **GlycA**: A novel marker for chronic inflammation and longevity, as highlighted in recent molecular medicine research.
- **CRP**: Should be kept as low as possible, ideally below 1.0 mg/L.
